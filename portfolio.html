THESIS FOCUS

Designing a sustainable city in the Sonipat area along the Yamuna River, integrating ecological sensitivity with urban development principles through comprehensive environmental assessment and spatial planning.

Research Methodology:

  • Ecological Capacity Assessment using GIS analysis
  • Land Suitability Analysis for sustainable development
  • Water Availability Assessment and management planning
  • Environmental Modelling (Hydrological, Ecosystem, Food web models)
  • Multi-criteria evaluation for optimal land-use planning

Eco-Sensitive Urban Design Framework

Project Scope:

  • Location: Sonipat area, Haryana (along the Yamuna River corridor)
  • Focus: Eco-sensitive urban design integrated with ecological sustainability
  • Integration: River ecology preservation with modern urban infrastructure
  • Approach: Multi-nuclei polycentric urban model for balanced development

Theoretical Framework - Multi-Nuclei Theory Application:

The thesis proposes a polycentric urban form that:

  • Reduces congestion by distributing activities across multiple nodes
  • Promotes balanced regional development and decentralization
  • Maintains ecological integrity of the Yamuna riverine ecosystem
  • Creates sustainable employment and residential nodes
  • Integrates green infrastructure with built environment
  • Implements ecological carrying capacity constraints in planning
  • Ensures resource availability for projected population growth

Key Environmental Considerations:

  • Biodiversity preservation and habitat protection zones
  • Water resource management and wetland conservation
  • Soil and ecological capacity assessment
  • Pollution level monitoring and environmental regulation
  • Climate-responsive sustainable construction practices
